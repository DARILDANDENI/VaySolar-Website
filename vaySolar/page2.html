Why Not Installing Solar in the Next 5 Years Might be your Biggest Mistake

Date/Time:



In our last post, we mentioned how photovoltaic cells, used in solar panels, convert sunlight to electricity to provide a sustainable source of electricity for our homes. Adoption of a sustainable, organic, and eco-friendly urban lifestyle is underway and investment in solar is taking center stage in the public, private, and residential segments. As we move away from fossil-generated electricity to sustainable renewable energy, it’s worth quantifying benefits that will accrue over the lifetime of a solar plant to a consumer.



The availability of a shadow-free area and the size of a solar plant are core determinants of how much electricity is generated by a solar system. Solar plants could be installed in areas starting from 100 square feet to an are as many as 100 acres, depending on the availability of such area. On average, 1 kWP (kilowatt peak) can be installed on 100 square feet of available area. Solar plant of 1 kWP can cost between ₹ 50,000 to ₹ 75,000 depending on the quality and technology of solar panels, materials used, AMC provided, etc. by the installation partner.



For residential customers, Ministry of New and Renewable Energy (MNRE) provides 40% subsidy on the benchmark cost of plants up to 3 kW and 20% on additional capacity up to 10 kW. Under the new procedure, this subsidy would directly come to a customer’s bank account upon submission of relevant documentation and approvals. A consumer can choose to send the surplus electricity generated by the solar plant back to the grid and paying electricity bills for only net usage. This is enabled by net meters installed by electricity distribution companies (DISCOM).

Net-metering makes solar installation highly lucrative for customers in two ways: 1.Customers get continued electricity even on days with insufficient generation via panels. 2.The excess electricity generated is netted out of the total electricity consumed, ensuring that the generated electricity is setoff against the highest-paid slab tariff.



Additionally, consumers also save an extra 20% tax on electricity which is paid on top 9 of slab tariffs.



As customers pay high electricity bills, Solar Plants become more and more lucrative as a long-term investment owing to the minimal cost of production from solar panels. This is driven mostly by the low cost of energy production with solar. With subsidies, residential customers can achieve the lowest cost of energy consumption over a period of 25 years.
